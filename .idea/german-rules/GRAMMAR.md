## Not Ported Rules (300+)

### ❌ NOT PORTED - High Priority (Common Grammatical Errors)

1. **VOR_ALLEN** - `vor allen → vor allem`
   - **Reason**: Fixed expression vs. literal meaning
   - **Example**: "vor allen Dingen" vs "vor allem"
   - **Difficulty**: Medium

2. **GROESSTER_ALS** - `größter als → größer als`
   - **Reason**: Comparative adjective agreement
   - **Example**: "Er ist größer als ich" (not "größter")
   - **Difficulty**: High

3. **HALBE_MILLIONEN** - `eine halbe Millionen → eine halbe Million`
   - **Reason**: Number agreement with "Million"
   - **Example**: "eine halbe Million Menschen"
   - **Difficulty**: High

4. **AUFGRUND_GENITIV** - `'aufgrund' benötigt Genitiv`
   - **Reason**: Case government (requires genitive after "aufgrund")
   - **Example**: "aufgrund des Wetters" (not "aufgrund dem Wetter")
   - **Difficulty**: Very High

5. **WERDEN_FOLGENDEN_SUB** - `werden folgenden → werden folgende`
   - **Reason**: Adjective agreement in future tense
   - **Example**: "werden folgende Dinge passieren"
   - **Difficulty**: High

6. **WELCHE_SUBMAS_IST** - `Welche der ... ist → sind`
   - **Reason**: Subject-verb agreement with relative pronoun
   - **Example**: "Welche der Fragen sind relevant?"
   - **Difficulty**: Very High

   - **Reason**: Adjective ending in accusative
   - **Example**: "Herzlichen Glückwunsch!"
   - **Difficulty**: Medium

8. **SCHOENE_WETTER** - `Es ist schöne Wetter → schönes Wetter`
   - **Reason**: Adjective ending with neuter nouns

9. **DU_KANNS** - `du kanns → du kannst`

## Major Challenges

### Linguistic Complexity

1. **Case Government**: Many rules require understanding which case a verb/preposition requires
2. **Adjective Endings**: Complex system of adjective declensions
3. **Verb Conjugation**: Different forms for each person/number
4. **Subject-Verb Agreement**: Complex agreement rules
5. **Passive Voice**: Different word order in passive constructions
6. **Subjunctive Mood**: Special verb forms for conditional/subjunctive

### Why Weir Format Struggles

1. **No Morphological Analysis**: Cannot understand verb forms, adjective endings
2. **No Syntactic Analysis**: Cannot parse sentence structure
3. **No Case Understanding**: Cannot handle German case system
4. **No Agreement Checking**: Cannot check subject-verb, adjective-noun agreement
5. **Limited Context**: Cannot analyze complex grammatical relationships

## Implementation Strategies

### Strategy 1: Fixed Expression Library

Create Weir rules for fixed expressions that are always wrong:
- `vom Beruf` → `von Beruf`
- `seit Anfang an` → `von Anfang an`
- `auf jedem Fall` → `auf jeden Fall`

### Strategy 2: Number Context

Use surrounding numbers to trigger rules:
- `nach [number] Tage` → `nach [number] Tagen`
- `eine halbe Millionen` → `eine halbe Million`

### Strategy 3: Simple Patterns

Focus on patterns that don't require full grammatical analysis:
- Simple adjective endings in predictable contexts
- Common verb conjugation errors
- Fixed preposition-expression combinations
